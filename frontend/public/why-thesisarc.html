The Investor's Dilemma

You Buy on a Hunch

A tip from a friend, a hot take on Twitter, a stock your advisor mentioned—but you never write down *why* you actually bought it.

No Accountability

Without a thesis, you panic-sell on bad news or hold too long hoping it bounces back. You're flying blind.

Information Overload

Bloomberg terminal? $20k/year. Generic news feeds? Noise. You need signal scoped to what you actually own.

Blind to Your Biases

Anchoring bias, recency bias, overconfidence—they're already costing you money before you even know they exist.

No Feedback Loop

How well is your thesis holding up? Are the news and fundamentals still supporting your original case? You don't track it.

Invisible Risk

You don't see the health of your entire portfolio at a glance. One thesis failure cascades without warning.
