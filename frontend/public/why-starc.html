A Week with STARC

Here's how a typical investor uses STARC through a real market week.

Monday — Morning Briefing flags a bearish signal

Your briefing shows MSFT news: "Microsoft delays Copilot enterprise rollout due to integration issues." STARC maps it to your thesis point "AI integration across enterprise is a growth catalyst" and marks it bearish. Score drops from 52 → 35. Zone shifts to Breaking.

Tuesday — Clicking ℹ on the at-risk thesis point

You click the ℹ icon next to the broken thesis point. STARC opens an AI chat pre-loaded with context. You ask: "Is this delay structural or a one-quarter blip?" The AI references filing data and explains it looks temporary. You decide to dispute the signal and lock the point as a core conviction.

Wednesday — Re-evaluation: Score Delta +8

You run a fresh evaluation. The Score Delta panel shows +8: 1 recovered point (your locked conviction held), 1 newly confirmed point from an Azure earnings beat. MSFT climbs back to 43 — still cautious, but no longer at risk. You breathe easier.

Thursday — Screener surfaces a new idea

You open the Screener and swipe through a few cards. A semiconductor stock you hadn't considered appears. You swipe up to add to your watchlist. STARC pre-fills thesis templates based on your Growth archetype. You customize 3 points and save.

Friday — 5-day streak, quiz reinforces your NVDA conviction

Your streak badge glows orange — 5 consecutive evaluation days. The Thesis Quiz pops up: "Which statement is part of your NVDA thesis?" You pick correctly from 4 options. That thesis point — CUDA ecosystem entrenchment — is now locked in muscle memory, not just a note in an app.
