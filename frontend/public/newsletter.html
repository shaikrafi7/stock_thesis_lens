StARC

The arc of conviction, stress-tested daily.

A personal investment thesis tool that keeps you honest about why you own every stock in your portfolio.

The Problem

You bought a stock for good reasons. But over time, those reasons get buried.

  • You forget why you bought it
  • You react emotionally to price drops
  • You get overwhelmed by conflicting news
  • You hold losers too long and sell winners too early
STARC gives you a framework to write, organize, and stress-test your investment thesis — for every stock you own. You define the thesis. You choose what matters. You decide what to act on.

What Makes STARC Different

Your Thesis, Your Rules

For every stock, you build a thesis across six categories: Competitive Moat, Growth, Valuation, Financial Health, Ownership, and Risks. Start from an AI-drafted outline or write every point yourself. Edit anything. Add your own insights. Remove what doesn't fit.

The thesis is yours — AI just gets you started faster.

[ Screenshot: Thesis points with categories, checkboxes, importance icons ]

Transparent, Rules-Based Scoring

No mysterious "AI scores." STARC's scoring is fully transparent: base score of 50, confirmed points add credit, flagged points apply deductions. Each category has a known weight. You can trace every point in your score back to a specific market signal.

No black boxes. You always know why your score is what it is.

Daily Briefing, Filtered For You

Every day, STARC surfaces only news relevant to your specific holdings. Each item is tagged with impact — bullish, bearish, or neutral — and linked to the thesis points it affects. 30 seconds to stay informed.

[ Screenshot: Morning briefing with impact-tagged news items ]

Multiple Portfolios

Organize investments into separate portfolios — "Core Holdings", "Tech Growth", "Speculative" — each with independent thesis tracking, scoring, and briefings. Switch instantly.

[ Screenshot: Portfolio switcher dropdown ]

Lock Your Core Convictions

This is the feature that changes how you invest. Freeze any thesis point to mark it as a core conviction — the fundamental reason you own the stock.

  • Preserved when you regenerate the thesis
  • Cannot be accidentally deselected
  • Carry a 2x penalty if broken by market evidence
  • Trigger a red alert: "Core Conviction Under Pressure"

If the core reason you bought the stock no longer holds, STARC makes sure you know — clearly and immediately.

How Scoring Works

Fully transparent. Fully deterministic. No hidden judgments.

CategoryCreditDeductionWhy
Competitive Moat+8.0-8.0Core thesis — highest impact
Risks & Bear Case+4.0-7.0Risks materializing hurts more
Growth Trajectory+6.0-6.0Growth signals carry real weight
Valuation+5.0-5.0Fair value affects score moderately
Financial Health+5.0-5.0Balance sheet is a steady factor
Ownership+4.0-4.0Supporting evidence

Importance multipliers: Standard 1x · Important 1.5x · Critical 2x · Frozen 2x

Example: AAPL with 12 selected points

After evaluation, 4 points are confirmed (market supports them), 1 frozen point is flagged, 7 have no strong signal.

Credits: +6.6 + 6.4 + 3.0 = +16.0
Deductions: -13.6 (frozen moat break: 8.0 × 0.85 × 2.0)

Score = 50 + 16.0 - 13.6 = 52.4 (Under Pressure)

One frozen break nearly wiped out all credit from three confirmed points. That's the power of core convictions.

Portfolio score = simple average of all stock scores. Three stocks at 52, 81, 68 = 67.0 (Holding).
