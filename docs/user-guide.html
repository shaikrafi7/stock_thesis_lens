1. Getting Started

Creating an Account

Navigate to ThesisArc and click Sign Up. Enter your email, choose a username, and set a password (minimum 6 characters). You'll be automatically logged in after registration.

Logging In

Use the Sign In tab to enter your email and password. Your session persists until you log out or your token expires.

First Look

After logging in, you'll see the Dashboard — the central hub showing your portfolio health, stocks, and key metrics. On first use it will be empty. Start by adding your first stock.

2. Managing Portfolios

ThesisArc supports multiple portfolios so you can organize investments however you like.

Your Default Portfolio

Every account starts with a Default portfolio. You can rename it but not delete it. All stocks go here unless you switch to a different portfolio.

Creating a New Portfolio

  1. Click the portfolio name in the top header bar (e.g., "Default")
  2. A dropdown appears showing all your portfolios
  3. Click + New Portfolio at the bottom
  4. Type a name and click Add
  5. You'll automatically switch to the new portfolio

Switching Portfolios

Click the portfolio name in the header and select any portfolio from the dropdown. The entire dashboard updates to reflect the selected portfolio.

Deleting a Portfolio

Hover over a non-default portfolio in the dropdown and click the trash icon. This permanently removes the portfolio and all its stocks, theses, and evaluations.

Note: The Default portfolio cannot be deleted. You can rename it by editing its name.

3. Adding Stocks

Single Stock

In the Portfolio Stocks section, type a ticker (e.g., AAPL) and click + Add. ThesisArc will:

  1. Look up the company info (name, sector, logo)
  2. Auto-generate an AI thesis with ~18 points across 6 categories
  3. Run an initial evaluation and score

Multiple Stocks

Enter comma-separated tickers: AAPL, NVDA, MSFT. Each stock is added and evaluated sequentially.

Removing a Stock

In the stocks table, click the trash icon on the stock's row. The stock and all its thesis data are removed from the portfolio.

The large semicircle gauge at the top shows your portfolio's average thesis score on a 0-100 scale.

Portfolio Stocks Table

A sortable list of all stocks in your active portfolio:

  • Logo & Ticker — click to open the stock detail page
  • Sparkline — 1-year price trend at a glance
  • Score — latest thesis evaluation (X/100)
  • Freshness — how recently evaluated (green = fresh, yellow = aging, red = stale)
  • Trend — score direction since last evaluation
  • Status Badge — green / yellow / red

Click Ticker or Score headers to sort.

Sector Breakdown

A donut chart showing which sectors your portfolio covers, with tickers listed per sector.

Portfolio Returns

Compares performance against the S&P 500 benchmark:

  • Period selector: 1M, 3M, 6M, 1Y
  • Return gauge with dollar equivalent ($10K starting)
  • Alpha: your return minus the benchmark
  • Per-stock breakdown: expand to see individual returns

Evaluate All

Click Evaluate All to re-score every stock in your portfolio at once. Results show counts of evaluated, skipped, and errored stocks.

5. Morning Briefing

An AI-generated daily summary of news relevant to your portfolio stocks.

What It Shows

  • Summary — brief overview of the day's market narrative
  • News items tagged by ticker and impact:
    • Bullish — positive for your thesis
    • Bearish — potentially damaging
    • Neutral — informational, no clear impact
  • Source links — click to read the full article
  • Thesis suggestions — some items offer a point you can add with one click

Refreshing

Click the refresh button to generate a new briefing. Briefings are cached daily — refreshing forces a new generation.

History

Expand the history section below today's briefing to see past briefings (up to 7 days).

7. Thesis Management

The thesis is the core of ThesisArc — a structured set of statements about why you own a stock.

Six Categories

CategoryWhat It Covers
Competitive MoatBrand strength, network effects, switching costs, patents
Growth TrajectoryRevenue growth, market expansion, new products
ValuationP/E ratios, price targets, relative value
Financial HealthCash flow, debt levels, profitability
Ownership & ConvictionInsider buying, institutional ownership, analyst consensus
Risks & Bear CaseCompetition, regulation, execution risks

Generating a Thesis

When you add a stock, ThesisArc auto-generates ~18 thesis points using AI. Click Regenerate Thesis for fresh points.

On regeneration: Manual points and frozen points are preserved. Only AI-generated unfrozen points are replaced.

Selecting Points

Each point has a checkbox. Only selected points are included in evaluation. Minimum 3 required. Use Select All / Deselect All to toggle quickly.

Adding Manual Points

Click the + on any category header. Type at least 10 characters and click Add. Manual points are preserved on regeneration.

Editing & Deleting

Hover over any point to reveal action buttons: edit (pencil), delete (X). Frozen points require confirmation before deletion.

Importance Levels

IconLevelEffect
—StandardNormal weight in scoring
StarImportantHigher weight in scoring
LightningCriticalHighest weight in scoring

Freezing Core Convictions

Click the lock icon to freeze a thesis point. Frozen points are your core convictions.

  • Cannot be deselected
  • Preserved on thesis regeneration
  • Carry 2x penalty if broken during evaluation
  • Trigger a red alert banner if broken
Core Conviction Alert: When a frozen point breaks, a red banner appears: "Core Conviction Under Pressure." This is the strongest sell signal ThesisArc produces.

8. Evaluation & Scoring

Evaluation tests your thesis against current market reality.

Running an Evaluation

Click Evaluate (X selected). The AI analyzes each selected thesis point against market data, news, and fundamentals, then scores 0-100.

Understanding Results

Evaluate All

From the dashboard, click Evaluate All to re-score every stock in your portfolio at once.

9. AI Assistant

A conversational AI accessible via the floating button in the bottom-right corner.

Research AI (Stock Detail Page)

Context-aware conversations about the specific stock you're viewing.

  • "What's the biggest risk for this stock?"
  • "Suggest a growth catalyst"
  • "How does the valuation compare to peers?"

Can suggest thesis points — a card appears with Add and Dismiss buttons.

Portfolio AI (Dashboard)

Conversations about your entire portfolio.

  • "Which stock is weakest right now?"
  • "Add Microsoft to my portfolio"
  • "Suggest a risk point for NVDA"

Can add/remove stocks and suggest thesis points for any stock.

Chat History

Conversations are saved per-stock and per-portfolio. Previous conversations are restored when you return.

10. Tips & Best Practices

Build a Strong Thesis

  • Be specific — "Revenue growing 15% YoY" beats "Company is growing"
  • Cover all categories — a thesis with only growth points ignores risk
  • Add manual points — AI gets you started, your insights matter most
  • Review regularly — theses should evolve as the company does

Use Freezing Strategically

  • Freeze only true core convictions — the 2-3 reasons you'd never sell
  • A frozen break is the strongest sell signal ThesisArc produces
  • Don't freeze everything — it defeats the purpose

Stay Consistent

  • Check the Morning Briefing daily — 30 seconds keeps you informed
  • Re-evaluate weekly — scores drift as conditions change
  • Act on red flags — if a stock stays "At Risk" across evaluations, revisit your thesis honestly

Organize with Portfolios

  • Separate core holdings from speculative positions
  • Use descriptive names: "Retirement Core", "Tech Growth", "Earnings Plays"
  • Each portfolio gets its own briefing and scoring — keep them focused
